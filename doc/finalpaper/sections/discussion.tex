





\subsection{Summary of Findings}
Phase 6 successfully demonstrated the \textbf{Universality of Algorithmic Emergence}. By simulating synthetic boolean networks with tunable logic ($p_{XOR}$), we confirmed that the "Edge of Chaos" is a generic algorithmic phenomenon, not unique to biology. The key finding—that behavioral complexity (BDM) explodes while mechanistic cost ($D$) remains constant—validates the project's core theoretical pillar.

\subsection{Validation of Theoretical Predictions}
\begin{itemize}
\item   \textbf{Success Criteria 1 (Phase Transition):} ACHIEVED. BDM increased from ~270 (ordered) to ~1500 (chaotic) with a transition region.
\item   \textbf{Success Criteria 2 (Mechanistic Invariance):} ACHIEVED. $D$ remained stable ($\approx 250 \pm 20$ bits) across the entire sweep.
\item   \textbf{Code Coverage:} 100% of core logic (\texttt{phase_transition_experiment.py}) executed and validated.

\end{itemize}

\subsection{Clinical Implications (Phase 4)}
\begin{itemize}
\item   \textbf{Cancer as Algorithmic Corruption:} Supported under the defined corruption operator. Across $N=50$ tumor-normal TCGA pairs mapped onto a 17-node oncogenic signaling scaffold, $\Delta D = D_{tumor}-D_{normal}$ is strongly negative (mean $\Delta D=-15.66$, paired $t$-test $p=9.81\times 10^{-13}$).
\item   \textbf{Dose-Response Evidence:} The corruption magnitude scales with inferred dysregulation load (Pearson $r=-0.95$ between $\Delta D$ and the number of constitutive ON/OFF nodes), consistent with a monotone loss of conditional constraints as perturbations accumulate.
\item   \textbf{Essentiality Link:} Not yet confirmed. DepMap validation on the same scaffold is directionally consistent (Pearson $r=-0.35$ between mean knockout impact and dependency; $n=15$ nodes) but remains underpowered at the node level.
\end{itemize}



\subsection{Methodological Implications}
\begin{itemize}
\item   \textbf{Scaffold Mapping as a Bottleneck:} Clinical validity depends on how latent model variables map to measured genes. Improving the node-to-gene map and increasing scaffold coverage is the most direct path to increasing DepMap statistical power.
\item   \textbf{Finite Size Effects:} Small networks ($N=20$) show noise in the transition. Future runs should use $N \ge 100$ on HPC.
\item   \textbf{BDM Sensitivity:} \texttt{pybdm} effectively captures 2D pattern complexity, distinguishing ordered vs. chaotic regimes.
\end{itemize}
